Framing the problem

"Patient wait times are too long"

1. The nurses are burnt out; give them more PTO

2. The nurses are not being compensated fairly; increase their salaries or offer additional benefits

3. The nurses do not have a proper support system; improve existing training programs or implement new programs or opportunities to support career advancements

"DocCharts was a failure"

1. The implementation of DocCharts was poorly planned; In the future, spend more time consulting with internal stakeholders and vendor consults before implementation

2. Our staff did not have a support system in place to facilitate the usability of DocCharts; spend more time training staff or use a 'cascading' type of implementation

3. Comprehensive card was not ready to implement DocCharts; rather than rushing the implementation, spend more time assessing the readiness of staff and start the implementation slowly

4. DocCharts did not suit comprehensive care's workflow; explore other EHR vendors

"Patient wait times are too long"

1. Our current system for handling patient's is not efficient; explore different workflows and study healthcare systems that handle large patient volumes and try to use parts of their system that may be transferrable to your setting

2. We do not have the stadding numbers or resources to support our patient workload; increase staff count or decrease the number of consults

3. Our patients are not educated enough or have expectations that do not match the reality of our care setting; Make these wait times transparent with patients (i.e. Use historical wait time data to provide estimates on the clinic website to set expectations for patients before they visit)

4. Patients aren't aware of our alternatives to in-person care; promote telehealth!

Week 5 Activity

  • Company: Allianz SE

    Online/mobile claim submission process is clunky, unreliable and inconvenient for customers

  • How might we...

    • How might we revamp the claim submission steps to enhance ease of use for members?
    • How might we improve the photo evidence submission process for our members?
    • How might we improve member education of the claim submission process for members?

  • Evaluating the chosen question

    • Is the issue addressed by your question important? - YES
    • Do you have control or influence over the problem? - YES
    • Does your questions require imagination to answer? - Not Necessarily
    • Does it inspire you to find many ideas? - YES

  • Reverse-brainstorming solutions

    • Increase the amount of typing/writing required to make the claim submission process more time consuming and tedious
    • Remove the option for image capture from the claim submission process, forcing members to rely solely on text-based descriptions or documentation
    • Create a policy in which a member needs to complete a claim form for each expense they try to claim
    • Make sure that member support lines or chats are only available 2 hours a day, between business hours
    • Remove all affiliations/partnerships with providers to ensure that members need to complete the claim submission instead of direct billing
    • Once a claim is submitted, provide no feedback and auto-deny any claims that did not provide the appropriate documentation
    • Force members to rely solely on paperwork and documentation for claim submission, causing inconvenience and loss of evidence

  • Potential solutions reversed

    • Use smort-form/pre-population tech to reduce the amount of typing/writing required
    • Integrate image capture into the claim submission process
    • Skip the claim form altogether and use the data from documents submitted by the member
    • Offer real-time live-chat/customer support in the claim submission page
    • Integrate a barcode feature so providers can scan the code and retrieve the member's information immediately and submit a claim quickly without any work required by the member
    • Use AI tech that will automatically provide feedback during the claim submission process (i.e. pictures too blurry, missing docs etc.)
    • Implement a "record" feature that allows members to record their interaction with their provider to eliminate any paperwork required for a submission

  • Solution: AI tech to provide auto feedback

    The proposed solution involves leveraging AI technology to automatically provide feedback during the claim submission process. In a standard medical claim submission, feedback on the submission is only provided once an examiner has had the opportunity to review the submission. This can push reimbursement back days to weeks if there is missing information that needs to be requested. In the proposed solution, when members complete the steps to submitting a medical claim, the AI system integrated into the online portal will auto-review the submission as it's being performed and provide feedback. For instance, if the system detects unclear images of supporting docs, the system will notify the member to re-submit the documents.
